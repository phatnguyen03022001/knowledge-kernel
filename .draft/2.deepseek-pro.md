## Why this is the most canonical structure?

### 1. Clear separation between **Knowledge** and **Operations**

| Directory | Nature | For whom? |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| `foundation/` | Knowledge about how to organize knowledge | Readers, system |
| `governance/` | Rules and processes for knowledge governance | Administrators, readers |
| `operations/` | Execution tools (ACP) | Devs, AI agents |
| `knowledge/` | Domain knowledge (SSOT, models, schemas) | System, users, devs |
| `archive/` | Knowledge history | Readers, audit |

- `operations/` doesn't "pollute" `governance/` (avoids conflating rules and tools).
- `governance/` stays purely about **rules**, no machine config files.
- `knowledge/` remains the heart, unmixed with anything operational.

### 2. Reflects the correct logical flow

```text
Foundation (thinking foundation)
    ↓
Governance (rules of the game)
    ↓
Operations (tools that enforce rules)
    ↓
Knowledge (knowledge protected by the layers above)
    ↓
Archive (history of what changed)
```

This embodies:

> *Reality → Knowledge → Representation → Governance → History*

but concretized into a system capable of **self-protection** and **self-evolution**.

### 3. Makes no assumptions about technology or moment

- No `ai/`, `rag/`, `infra/`, `ops/`, `roadmap/`, `product/`.
- `operations/acp-packs/` can be replaced by another protocol in the future without affecting `knowledge/`.
- YAML files in `schemas/` work with any AI engine, not tied to a specific framework.

### 4. Satisfies all core principles

| Principle | Expressed in structure |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Git is SSOT** | The entire tree is a Git repo |
| **Contract over prompt** | `schemas/*.yaml` are contracts; ACP packs are operation contracts |
| **Validator is law** | `validator-config.yaml` + invariants in each pack |
| **Stateless execution** | Each pack is self-contained, session-independent |
| **No AI has memory** | Cold-start guarantee: `00-index.md` + `layer_zero` in packs |
| **Pure knowledge** | `knowledge/` contains zero traces of technology |

## Differences from previous structure

Previously you considered placing ACP under `governance/acp-packs/`.  
After counterargument, I choose to **separate `operations/` entirely** because:

- **Governance** = rules (for humans to read).
- **Operations** = tools that enforce rules (for machines to read).

Keeping them together causes ambiguity at scale. Separating them:
- New team members won't confuse policy documents with config files.
- Operations tools can change (e.g., swap ACP for BCP) without touching governance.
- Reflects reality: Governance **delegates to** Operations, not contains Operations.

## Conclusion

This is the **most canonical** structure for a pure Knowledge OS, capable of being operated by multi-agent AI, but unbound by any specific technology or moment.

It is the perfect intersection of:

> **Knowledge** (what endures)  
> and  
> **Protocol** (how knowledge is created, verified, and changed).

And most importantly: **it doesn't freeze**. `operations/` can evolve, `knowledge/` can expand, `governance/` can adjust. But this foundational structure is robust enough to withstand that change.
